How is this calculated?

The Rule of 25

Your Freedom Number = Desired Monthly Income x 12 months x 25. This is the total invested amount needed so that a 4% annual withdrawal covers your expenses indefinitely. It is the most widely used benchmark in early retirement planning.

The 8% Return

We use 8% average annual return based on the S&P 500 historical performance since 1957. This is a pre-tax, nominal figure. Actual returns vary year to year. Some years are negative, some are 20%+. Over 20+ year horizons, the average converges toward this range.

What This Does NOT Include

This calculator does not deduct taxes, investment fees, or inflation from projections. Real-world returns after these factors are typically 5 to 7%. Your actual timeline may be longer. This is a directional tool, not a guarantee. The point: see your target, then build the vehicle to get there.

How Accurate Is This?

Over short periods (1 to 5 years), projections are unreliable. Markets are volatile. Over long periods (15 to 30+ years), compound growth at 7 to 10% annually has held remarkably consistent in US equity markets. The longer your horizon, the more reliable the math becomes.
