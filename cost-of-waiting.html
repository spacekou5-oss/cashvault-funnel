How This Works

Your money earns money. That money earns more money. When you wait, you lose the earliest years of this chain reaction. Those earliest years are worth the most because they have the longest time to compound. One year of delay doesn't cost you one year of growth. It costs you the growth ON the growth ON the growth of that year. That's why a small wait creates a massive gap.

The S&P 500 has averaged roughly 10% annually before inflation, roughly 7-8% after inflation, over the past 50+ years. We use 8% as the pre-tax nominal benchmark. Your actual returns will vary year to year. Some years negative, some years 20%+. But over 20+ year windows, the average converges. This is the same assumption used by every retirement calculator on the internet. We just show you the cost of NOT using it.
