Beehiiv Email Infrastructure
The central nervous system of your entire revenue model. Every dollar you will ever make flows through email first.
Infrastructure Build Once
Build: 1.5 hrs
Maintain: 0 hrs/wk
Tool Stack
Beehiiv Claude (DNS troubleshooting)
What This System Does

Creates the foundation that every email automation in your business depends on. Without a verified Beehiiv account with correct domain settings and subscriber tagging, Systems 3, 7, 14, 15, 16, and 17 all fail. This is the single most critical dependency in the entire blueprint.

How to Build
  • Create Beehiiv account. Use your primary email. Select the free tier (covers up to 2,500 subscribers, which lasts through Phase 1 and most of Phase 2).
  • Verify sending domain. If using a custom domain: add the 3 DNS records Beehiiv provides (SPF, DKIM, DMARC). If not: confirm the default Beehiiv sending address. Custom domain recommended because it improves deliverability and brand recognition.
  • Create subscriber tags. Set up these tags now (you will use them across all future systems): source-freedom-calc, source-cost-of-waiting, source-800-club, source-autopilot, source-prompt-vault, source-budget-calc, source-investment-simp, source-manychat, mvo-kit, mvo-pro, mvo-blueprint, buyer-bronze, buyer-silver, inactive-30d
  • Send yourself a test email. Create a blank broadcast. Send to yourself. Confirm it lands in your primary inbox, NOT spam. If it lands in spam, your domain DNS records are misconfigured. Fix before proceeding.
  • Add a test subscriber manually. Use a secondary email. Apply the tag "source-freedom-calc". Confirm the tag is visible in the subscriber profile. This proves tagging works.
Working

Test email in your inbox (not spam). Test subscriber visible with correct tag in Beehiiv dashboard. Account shows "verified" status.

Broken

Test email in spam folder (DNS records wrong). Tags not appearing on subscriber (setup error). Account flagged or unverified.

A living document that maps what everyone else does so you can do what nobody does.
Document Update Quarterly
Build: 2 hrs
Maintain: 1 hr/quarter

Google Sheets Instagram (manual research) Claude (analysis)
What This System Does

Produces a gap analysis that directly informs your MVO concepts in System 12. Without this, your 3 product concepts are guesses. With it, they are bets placed against a mapped terrain. The deliverable is a Google Sheet with competitor data and a 1-paragraph attack vector statement.

How to Build
  • Create a Google Sheet with columns: Account Name | Follower Count | Content Format (video/text/carousels) | What They Sell (free/low-ticket/high-ticket) | Price Points | CTA Structure (bio link > where?) | Pain They Address | Engagement Feel (active comments vs. ghost) | Unique Angle | Gap (what they are NOT doing)
  • Identify 8-12 Gen Z finance creators. Search Instagram for #genzmoney, #moneytok, #financialliteracy. Find accounts between 1K-100K followers (your competitive band). Exclude anyone above 500K (different game).
  • Fill in the sheet for each account. Spend no more than 10 minutes per account. You are mapping the terrain, not studying for an exam.
  • Write the attack vector. At the bottom of the sheet, one paragraph: "Every competitor does X. Nobody does Y. Our attack vector is Y." This Y must be specific. "Better content" is not an attack vector. "Interactive personalized tools that calculate YOUR number, not generic advice" is.
  • Bonus: flag 3-5 collaboration targets. Creators at your level or slightly above who share your audience but do not compete directly. These become outreach targets for Phase 3.
Working

8+ competitors documented. Attack vector statement is specific and actionable. Sheet saved and accessible. MVO concepts in System 12 can directly reference this data.

Broken

Fewer than 6 competitors analyzed (not enough data). Attack vector is vague ("be different" tells you nothing). No price point data collected (you cannot position your MVO without knowing the market).

Research Accelerator "You are a competitive intelligence analyst for a Gen Z personal finance brand targeting 19-24 year olds earning $14-22/hr. Here is my competitive data on [X] accounts: [paste sheet contents]. Analyze: (1) What format patterns dominate? (2) What price points cluster together? (3) What pain points are oversaturated vs. underserved? (4) Where is the whitespace nobody is filling? Write a 1-paragraph attack vector statement."

3-Email Welcome Sequence
The trust-building machine. Every new subscriber enters this sequence automatically for the rest of time.
Automation Runs Forever
Build: 3 hrs
Maintain: 0 hrs/wk
Tool Stack
Beehiiv (automation builder) Claude (heaviest usage in blueprint)
What This System Does

Converts a cold email address into a warm relationship over 5 days. Every person who enters their email on any of your 7 tools automatically receives 3 emails: immediate value delivery, Day 2 deeper insight, Day 5 relationship bridge or soft offer. This sequence runs without you. It is the trust engine that makes System 14 (Launch Emails) and System 15 (Evergreen Funnel) possible.

How to Build
  • In Beehiiv, create an Automation. Trigger: "New subscriber added." This fires for EVERY new subscriber regardless of source.
  • Email 1 (Immediate): Subject line includes the specific tool they used (use merge tag if available, or default to: "Your number is ready"). Body: deliver the result/insight they signed up for. Short. Personal. End with a question that invites a reply ("Hit reply and tell me: what surprised you most about your number?"). Replies train the algorithm that your emails are conversations, not spam.
  • Email 2 (Day 2 delay): Subject: something that creates curiosity about the math behind their result. Body: go deeper on the system. Show the compound math. Show the Operational Transparency (Glass Box: "Here's exactly how I calculated that number"). End with a bridge to a second tool ("Now that you know your Freedom Number, here's what it costs you every day you don't act on it:" + Cost of Waiting link).
  • Email 3 (Day 5 delay): Subject: relationship bridge. Body: your story. Why you are building this. The Blue Tape. The $1M countdown. Human connection. End with ONE CTA: either a soft product offer (if MVO is live) or a prompt to explore another tool. Single button. Not multiple links.
  • Test the entire sequence. Subscribe with a test email. Confirm Email 1 arrives immediately. Wait and confirm Email 2 arrives on Day 2. Confirm Email 3 arrives on Day 5. Check rendering on mobile.
Working

All 3 emails fire on schedule. Open rate above 50% on Email 1. Emails render correctly on mobile. At least 1 reply received from early subscribers (proves engagement).

Broken

Emails not firing (automation not published or trigger misconfigured). Open rate below 30% (subject lines are generic, rewrite). Emails in spam (deliverability issue, check System 01 domain settings).
