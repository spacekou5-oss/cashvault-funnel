As promised, here are the 7 Money Rules that build real wealth. The full PDF is also waiting for you in your email.

1. Pay Yourself First — The Exact Formula

Wealthy people save before they spend. On payday, immediately move 10-20% of your paycheck to a separate high-yield savings account BEFORE paying any bills.

Action: Set up an automatic transfer for every payday. A $2,000 paycheck means $200 instantly goes to your "Future You" account.
2. The 50/30/20 Budget Formula

Split your after-tax income: 50% for NEEDS (rent, groceries, utilities), 30% for WANTS (dining out, hobbies), and 20% for FINANCIAL GOALS (savings, investments, extra debt payments).

Action: Track with a free app like Mint. If your "Needs" are over 50%, your housing or transport costs are too high.
3. The 1/3 Housing Rule

Your rent/mortgage should not exceed 33% of your monthly take-home pay. This is the #1 rule to avoid being "house poor."

Action: If you take home $4,000/month, your max housing cost is $1,333. Any higher and you must increase income or lower housing costs.
4. The 3-Step Emergency Fund Process

Step 1: Save $1,000 FAST. Step 2: Calculate 3-6 months of essential living expenses. Step 3: Save that full amount in a high-yield savings account.

Action: Keep this fund at a separate bank (like Ally or Marcus) so you're not tempted to touch it.
5. The 20/4/10 Car Buying Rule

To avoid being "car poor": Put down at least 20%, finance for no more than 4 years, and ensure your total monthly car payment (including insurance) is under 10% of your gross income.

Action: Better yet, buy a reliable used car with cash and invest the difference.
6. The "Start Small, Start Now" Investing System

Do not wait to invest. Start with $25/week in a broad, low-cost market index fund (like VTI or VOO). Time in the market is more important than timing the market.

Action: Open a Roth IRA with Vanguard, Fidelity, or Schwab and set up an automatic weekly investment.
7. The 10-Year Wealth Mindset Shift

Before any major purchase, ask: "Will this asset appreciate or depreciate?" The wealthy buy assets that make them money (investments, skills). The poor buy liabilities that cost them money (luxury cars, consumer goods).

Action: Choose to upgrade your assets (investments) before you upgrade your lifestyle.
