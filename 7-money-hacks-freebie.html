Your Guide to the 7 Money Rules

Welcome! You've made a smart decision to take control of your finances. Below are the foundational rules to get you started on your journey to building real wealth.

Wealthy people save before they spend, not after. On payday, immediately move a portion of your income to savings/investments BEFORE paying any bills or expenses. Start with just 10% if you're a beginner.

Example: A $2,000 paycheck means $200 goes directly to your savings account. Set up an automatic transfer for every payday.
2

The 50/30/20 Budget Formula (Step-by-Step)

Split your after-tax income: 50% for NEEDS (rent, groceries, utilities), 30% for WANTS (dining out, hobbies), and 20% for FINANCIAL GOALS (savings, investing, extra debt payments).

Example: A $3,000 take-home pay means $1,500 for needs, $900 for wants, and $600 for goals. Use a free app to track this.
3

The 1/3 Housing Rule (To Avoid Being "House Poor")

Your rent or mortgage payment should never exceed 33% of your monthly take-home pay. This is the single biggest key to ensuring you have money left over to save and invest.

Example: A $4,000 take-home pay means your absolute maximum housing cost should be $1,333.
4

The 3-Step Emergency Fund Process

Step 1: Save $1,000 as fast as you can. Step 2: Calculate 3-6 months of your essential living expenses. Step 3: Save that full amount in a separate, high-yield savings account.

Example: If your monthly expenses are $2,500, your target emergency fund is between $7,500 and $15,000.
5

The 20/4/10 Car Buying Rule

To avoid being "car poor": Put down at least 20%, finance for no more than 4 years, and ensure your total car payment (including insurance) is under 10% of your gross income.

Example: The best strategy is to buy a reliable used car with cash and invest what would have been your monthly payment.
6

The "Start Small, Start Now" Investing System

Don't wait until you're "rich" to invest. Start with $25/week in a broad, low-cost market index fund. Time in the market is more powerful than timing the market.

Example: Open a Roth IRA and set up an automatic weekly purchase of an S&P 500 index fund (like VOO or FXAIX).
7

The 10-Year Wealth Mindset Shift

Before any major purchase, ask yourself: "Will this help me build wealth, or just look wealthy?" The rich upgrade their assets (investments, skills) before they upgrade their lifestyle.

Example: Instead of an $800/month luxury car payment, drive a reliable used car and invest the difference. After 10 years, you could have over $138,000.
