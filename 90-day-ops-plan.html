Binding Constraint Analysis

Time is the bottleneck. At 5-8 hrs/week, you have 65-104 total hours over 90 days. That is the entire budget. Every hour spent on infrastructure is an hour NOT spent creating content. Every hour spent on content is an hour NOT spent building systems. The plan must sequence these so infrastructure comes first (Weeks 1-3), then content dominates (Weeks 4-13).

Traffic is the secondary bottleneck. 67 followers and 1-5 posts means the funnel is a pipe with no water. The most perfect email sequence in the world converts zero if nobody sees the content. After infrastructure locks in, 60-70% of all weekly hours must go to content creation and engagement.

$18/hr equivalence: Every hour you spend on this business must generate more future value than the $18 you could earn at your job. Infrastructure hours are worth ~$50-100/hr in future leverage. Content hours are worth ~$25-75/hr in audience building. Admin hours are worth $0. Delete admin ruthlessly.

The next 90 days are NOT about making money. They are about building proof. Proof that the tools work. Proof that the content converts attention to action. Chain of Verification // 3 Strongest Ways This Plan Could Be Wrong

RISK ANALYSIS

If we don't attack these head-on, the plan fails silently
Risk 1: The Time Budget Is Too Tight

Threat Level: HIGH. 5-8 hrs/week is razor thin. One bad week (exams, extra shifts, life chaos) and the content cadence breaks. Two missed weeks in a row kills momentum entirely. The algorithm punishes inconsistency.

Mitigation: The batch recording model is the insurance policy. If you record 4 videos on Saturday, you have 2 weeks of buffer even if the following Saturday is lost. Build a 2-week content buffer by Week 5 and NEVER let it drop below 1 week. If you have a surplus week (8 hrs instead of 5), record 6-8 videos instead of 4.

Worst case protocol: If you miss 2+ consecutive weeks, pause everything except engagement (15 min/day). Resume recording as soon as possible. Do NOT restart infrastructure work. The pipes are sealed.

Risk 2: Content Doesn't Convert to Email Captures

Threat Level: MEDIUM. Views without captures means one of three things: (1) the CTA is unclear, (2) the tool/keyword path is broken, (3) the audience doesn't want what you're offering.

Mitigation: Track capture rate from Week 4. If below 3% by Week 6 (captures/followers), diagnose in order: First, test the ManyChat path yourself (is it broken?). Second, watch your own video and ask: "Did I actually say the keyword clearly and explain what they get?" Third, try a different CTA format: "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send it" vs "DM me [KEYWORD]."

Worst case protocol: If captures are near-zero by Week 8, the problem is likely the CTA bridge (your videos don't make the tool sound valuable enough). Shift 2 of your 4 weekly videos to "live demo" format where you USE the tool on camera, showing the result. Proof beats promises.

Risk 3: Follower Growth Stalls Below Critical Mass

Threat Level: MEDIUM. Without 250+ followers by Week 9, the PMF test in Arc 3 won't generate enough data to be meaningful. You'll be testing with a sample size of 10 people, which proves nothing.

Mitigation: Engagement is the growth lever at this stage, not content quality. The algorithm rewards accounts that participate in their community. If growth stalls below 150 by Week 6, increase engagement time to 25-30 min/day and target commenting on LARGER accounts in your niche (10k-50k followers) where your comment is seen by their audience.

Worst case protocol: If below 200 followers by Week 9, extend Arc 2 by 3 weeks and delay the PMF test. The data will be garbage if the audience is too small. Better to delay and test with real numbers than rush and learn nothing.

    Sunday. Once a week. 15 minutes. That's it. Checking stats 3x/day is procrastination wearing a data costume.

    You have 67 followers. You don't need a Rube Goldberg machine. You need a pipe with no holes. Keep ManyChat flows simple: keyword > link. That's it until Phase 2.
  • Researching competitors for more than 2 hours total
    90 minutes. One gap sentence. Done. Competitor research is the #1 procrastination trap for builders. You learn more from posting 4 videos than from studying 40 competitors.
